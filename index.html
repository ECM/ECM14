ECM14

14th Estuarine and Coastal Modeling Conference (ECM14)

Monday, June 13, 2016
Tuesday, June 14, 2016
Wednesday, June 15, 2016
University of Rhode Island, Kingston, RI

Register »

The Estuarine and Coastal Modeling Conference provides a venue for commercial, academic, and government scientists and engineers from around the world to present and discuss the latest results and techniques in applied estuarine and coastal modeling.

Abstracts may be submitted for oral or poster presentations. Accepted presenters may also submit papers to a special issue of the Journal of Marine Science and Engineering (JMSE).

Registration

Registration should be done online at our registration webpage. You will need to specify your choice of presentation type (poster or talk) and submit an abstract if you are presenting. The registration fee includes a welcome social on Sunday as well as breakfast and lunch each day. For presenters, no fees must be paid until notice of abstract acceptance.

Topic Areas

Many topics areas in estuarine and coastal modeling can be discussed, including: Pollutant Transport and Water Quality Prediction • Coastal Response to Climate Change • Modeling Techniques and Sensitivity Studies • Model Assessment • Modeling Specific Estuarine and Coastal Systems • Visualization Analysis • Wave and Sediment Transport Modeling • Modeling of Chemicals and Floatables • Oil Spill Transport and Fate Modeling • Inverse Methods • Circulation Modeling • Facility Siting and CSO Studies • Data Assimilation • Nowcast/Forecast Modeling Systems • Coupled Models of Terrestrial, Atmospheric, Coastal Systems • Modeling Systems with Strong Buoyancy Forcing • Modeling of Coupled Systems • Risk Analysis (Nuclear Reactors, Flood Forecasting)

There will also be a special session on Coastal Resilience.

Venue

The conference will be held at the University of Rhode Island Kingston Campus. Lodging and shared meals at URI will promote a retreat-like environment for the meeting, providing ample opportunity for discussion and meeting new colleagues. Sessions will be held on the Kingston Campus at the College of Pharmacy, and on the Bay Campus at the Graduate School of Oceanography. Providence and Newport are both about 30 minutes away.
